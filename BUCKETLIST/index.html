My Bucket List: Goals & Dreams!

Develop a Complex Simulation Game

One of my massive software goals is to create a complex simulation game heavily inspired by masterpieces like Dwarf Fortress and RimWorld. I want to build a game engine that holds deep, emergent scenarios where the AI and systems interact in unpredictable ways.

Programming the logic, managing the memory for thousands of background events, and creating a world where every action has a chaotic or brilliant consequence is the ultimate test of my programming abilities.


Conquer That 4-Bit Computer

For the past few weekends, trying to build my own 4-bit computer on a breadboard has been driving me absolutely crazy. Dealing with the wiring, the logic gates, and the SR Latches has been a massive test of patience.

But getting this thing to finally work perfectly is high on my bucket list. I want to see those LEDs blink in perfect sequence, proving that I successfully designed and wired the CPU architecture myself. It will be the most satisfying victory.


Program a Compact, Modern Operating System

Moving up from hardware, I want to write my own Operating System from scratch. I am deeply inspired by the technical feat of Terry Davis and TempleOS—the idea that one person can build an entire ecosystem, from the kernel to the compiler, without relying on massive, bloated libraries.

My goal is to create a system that is compact yet highly functional to modern standards. I want to handle memory management and write a custom kernel that stays "close to the metal," keeping the system lightweight but capable of running real-world applications. By following this path of extreme DIY development, I will finally bridge the gap between the logic of my 4-bit hardware and the high-level user software layer.


Travel to Egypt for the Mummies and Artifacts

I need to step away from the computer screens eventually! Traveling to Egypt is a massive dream of mine. I want to see the ancient tombs, the pyramids, and get up close with the real mummies and historical artifacts.

There is something incredibly fascinating about ancient engineering and how they preserved their history. Standing inside those monuments would be an unforgettable, surreal experience.


Become the #1 74kg Junior in the Nation

Outside of tech, the gym is where I push my physical limits. Right now, I am standing at rank 27 out of 107 in the 74kg junior weight class. My ultimate athletic goal is to grind my way to the very top and become the strongest 74kg junior in the nation.

It's going to take relentless training, strict programming, and incredible mental toughness, but I am ready to put in the work to add those heavy kilos to my total and secure that #1 spot.


Return to College for Computer Engineering

Right now, I am focusing on Information Technology, but my fascination with breadboards, processors, and hardware systems has sparked a bigger ambition. After I finish my IT degree, I plan to go back to college for Computer Engineering to master the physical side of tech.

I don't just want to understand circuits; I want to design my own PCBs (Printed Circuit Boards) and build fully functional, professional-grade devices from the ground up. Having a background in both IT and Computer Engineering will give me the ultimate hybrid skillset—allowing me to bridge the gap between low-level hardware fabrication and high-level network and software architecture. I want to be the person who not only writes the code but also builds the very machine it runs on.
